Control and Instrumentation Engineer Internship

GULF JP UT Limited, Ayutthaya, Thailand, July 2024 — September 2024

/kənˈtrəʊl ənd ˌɪnstrəmenˈteɪʃən/

"GULF JP UT" is a branch of GULF Thailand, jointly developed and owned by an obvious Japanese company for the appr. $400,000,000 "U-Thai" power plant.

The plant is a 1,600 MW CCGT (Combined-Cycle-Gas-Turbine) mostly LNG/methane IPP responsible for roughly 3-5% of Thailand's power capacity.

As of writing (05.12.2025), it is the 5th biggest power plant in Thailand, tied alongside GULF JP NS (Nong Saeng).

-

Hydrophase interned there for 2 months, working mostly to debug and repair faulty control boards of various instruments throughout the power plant.

Being a CCGT, this meant many valve control boards. In fact, it was probably all valves... but electronics stems from the same fundamentals. Insight gained from this experience was used to develop the MYOLIGHT and then furthered to design far more complex boards. This, in addition to the Advanced PCB Design Training, has broadened Hydrophase's horizons as he has interacted with both users and designers of industrial, high-performance PCBs.

Many thanks to the senior operators and the three other interns for their in-depth knowledge of various engineering principles.

-

It must be said, however, that Mitsubishi DIASYS is egregiously outdated. By comparison, Siemens' software (SIMATIC) is actually desireable, although as one may come to find later... Hydrophase also despises their PADS PCB design software...
